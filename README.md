# Protection against virulent Clostridioides difficile infection is explained by the metabolic niche breadth of avirulent colonizing strains
## Overview
*Clostridioides difficile* infection (CDI) affects nearly 500,000 patients annually in the US, with recurrence in ~30% of cases. Working with 21 clinical MLST1 isolates from Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (ANI >99.84%), we show that select avirulent strains confer complete protection against the hypervirulent VPI10463 strain while others do not. Protection is independent of adaptive immunity, microbiome composition, and identifiable genomic determinants. BIOLOG phenotype microarray profiling across 2,185 strain-by-metabolite conditions reveals that protective strains occupy substantially broader metabolic niches than non-protective or virulent strains. A consumer-resource model parameterized from these data predicts competitive exclusion of VPI10463 by protective strains and coexistence with non-protective strains, consistent with in vivo qPCR strain tracking. These findings establish metabolic niche breadth as the primary determinant of inter-strain competitive dynamics and CDI protection.

```
data/raw/          Raw unprocessed data, never modified
```
